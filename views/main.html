While having a strong math background can be helpful, it's not always necessary to start learning computer science. Many introductory courses assume minimal math knowledge and focus on teaching fundamental programming concepts. However, as you progress to more advanced topics like algorithms, data structures, and machine learning, a basic understanding of math, particularly algebra and discrete mathematics, becomes increasingly important.

The best programming language to learn first depends on your goals, interests, and the applications you're interested in developing. Popular choices for beginners include Python, Java, and JavaScript, as they have straightforward syntax, extensive documentation, and versatile applications across various domains. It's often recommended to start with a language that aligns with your intended projects or career path.
